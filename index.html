01 — About

Data without integrity
is just noise.

During the COVID pandemic, I joined the JNTUH-affiliated Robotics Lab and led a team of four building AI systems for smart cities. The hardest part wasn't the models — it was the data. Finding high-quality, consistently labeled, audit-ready datasets for real-world problems felt nearly impossible.

That experience shaped everything. I became obsessed with the question: what does it actually take to make data trustworthy? Not just collected — but labeled consistently, validated rigorously, documented for traceability, and structured so that anyone downstream can trust it completely.

Today I build the annotation schemas, QA pipelines, and validation frameworks that close that gap — in pediatric clinical research, geospatial AI, and acoustic signal processing. Because when the labels are right and the quality checks hold, everything built on top actually works.
